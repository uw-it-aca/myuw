Husky Experience Toolkit

This toolkit is designed to help you make the most of your time at UW. The articles here address four interconnected dimensions of the Husky Experience: Know Yourself, Know the World, Make Your Way, and Weave it Together. Wherever you are in your university career, use this toolkit to challenge yourself, explore your options, and integrate all you are learning - your husky experience is more than a major!

Know Yourself

Know your interests and what engages you. Gain experiences that will test ideas you have about who you are. Build a supportive social network and make decisions in your best interest. Understand how tending to your wellness provides the foundation to success in all other activities.

    {{{ know-yourself }}}

Know the World

Know how your education relates to the world. Understand the relationship between major and career, and the relationships between different disciplines. Ensure you have the knowledge and skills to achieve academically and contribute to a changing world.

    {{{ know-world }}}

Make Your Way

Gather the tools and knowledge you need to intentionally construct a career. Identify your skills and learn to articulate them to potential employers. Build a professional network composed of the right people to support you on a path to success.

    {{{ make-your-way }}}

Weave it Together

Reflect on your experiences large and small, and draw meaningful connections between them. Work to see your experiences as a coherent whole.

{{{ weave-together }}}

Why We Made This Toolkit

We are a team of UW staff members who care about your success. We want to help you create the kind of Husky Experience that will serve you best. To do this, we gathered and curated advice from students, faculty, and staff from across UW on how to make the most of your time here.

The toolkit was curated by Michaelann Jundt of Undergraduate Academic Affairs, Janice Fournier and William Washington of UW Information Technology and Katy DeRosier from the Office of the Provost.

How it works

These articles are delivered here via MyUW so that all students have access to these ideas and resources. New articles are highlighted each week on the homepage to provide timely advice, but read and explore them anytime.

We plan to add new articles and resources each academic year.
